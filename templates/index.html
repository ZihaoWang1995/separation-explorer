Material explorer for separations

What is it?

The dataset below is generated by calculating three key performance indicators: initial Henry's constant, working capacity and point uptake from publicly available isotherms from the NIST ISODB. The points displayed are means of multiple values.

Using the separation explorer

Select the two gases to investigate from the drop-down list. Materials with available isotherms on these probes are then plotted in the graphs below. The color of the points is determined by number of available isotherms. Hover over a point to display the material name. If clicked, the adsorbent is highlighted in all graphs and the standard deviation of the calculation is displayed as dotted lines, as well as several details in the adjoining table.

The uptake graph shows the amount adsorbed a particular pressure while the working capacity is the difference between uptake at two pressures. The sliders below can be used to change the pressures for the uptake or for the working capacity. Graph only shows materials where the loading can be calculated.
